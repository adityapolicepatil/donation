Blood

Welcome to Donatory, where we help connect people to donate and make a difference.

Why Donate?

Every two seconds, someone in the world needs blood. Blood transfusions are critical for a variety of medical situations, including:

  • Accident and trauma victims
  • Cancer patients undergoing treatment
  • Individuals undergoing surgery
  • People with chronic illnesses like sickle cell disease
    • Blood cannot be manufactured and has a short shelf life. Donated blood is essential to ensure a constant supply for those in need. A single blood donation can be separated into its components (red blood cells, plasma, platelets) and used to help multiple people.

    Who Can Donate?

    Most healthy adults can donate blood. Here's a general overview of eligibility criteria:

  • Age: Typically between 17-75 years old (age may vary slightly by location)
  • Weight: Minimum weight requirement 45 kilograms
  • Health: Generally healthy with no recent illnesses or infections
  • Medications: Some medications may temporarily disqualify you
    • Not sure if you're eligible? Take our quick and easy online quiz to find out: Australian Red Cross Quiz
